# Business Context & Regulations

This document provides context on the business goals and regulatory requirements for the Agentive-OpenFinance-BAAS-LFI_role platform.

## Regulatory Framework
- **OPF CBUAE Regulations**: The platform must adhere strictly to the Open Finance regulations mandated by the Central Bank of the UAE.
- **Agent-FTE Compliance Validation**: The `COMPLIANCE_VALIDATION` Agent-FTE autonomously analyzes all PRDs and architectures to ensure strict alignment with CBUAE guidelines. It operates with decoupled IAM tokens to query real-time regulatory databases.
- **CBUAE Consent Management**: Comprehensive consent flows will be integrated to ensure that users maintain full control over what data is shared and with whom.

## Business Context & Scrum Automation
- **Decoupled Business Context**: Core banking capabilities are decoupled into independent domains, preventing tight coupling and facilitating the API-first OpenFinance transition.
- **Agent-FTE Scrum Automation**: Daily operations are managed by AI Agents operating via the `ScrumCeremoniesActivitiesImpl`. Agents autonomously conduct daily standups, backlog refinements, and architecture reviews, heavily reducing human operational overhead.
- **AI Cost Planning & Economics**: The `FteCostOptimizer` evaluates real-time token consumption (LLM inference costs). Agent-FTEs are allocated strict computational budgets. If an agent hits its token limit, the system gracefully traps the `InsufficientFteBudgetException` and prompts a Human-In-The-Loop (HITL) for a budget extension to prevent runaway cloud expenditures while ensuring top-tier AI productivity.
- **Autonomous Event Ingestion**: OpenFinance events generated by CBUAE systems are ingested seamlessly in real-time via Apache Kafka. The `AUTONOMOUS_INGESTION` Agent-FTE processes these events dynamically without waiting for scheduled batch jobs or synchronous REST triggers.
- **Token Management**: Centralized management for access and refresh tokens, tied to Consent Management protocols.
